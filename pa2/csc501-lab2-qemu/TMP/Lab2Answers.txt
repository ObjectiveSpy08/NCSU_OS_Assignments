a) Priority inversion solution: An interesting solution to the priority inversion problem involves randomly boosting the priorities of low-priority ready processes holding locks. This allows such processes to be granted CPU time needed to execute their critical sections. Once they are done executing in their critical sections they release the lokcs allowing the higher priority process to execute. This policy is used in Microsoft Windows (https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/desktop/ProcThread/priority-inversion).

A possible issue with this approach is that of writer starvation where once a reader starts reading and new reader processes keep coming so that the writer keeps waiting on the "resource" forever.
